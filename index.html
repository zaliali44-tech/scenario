Formula Glossary

Reference
Workload Hours

Total demand converted into handling hours based on forecasted volume and AHT.

Workload Hours = (Volume × AHT in seconds) ÷ 3600
Net Productive Factor

Represents the portion of paid time available for productive work after shrinkage.

Net Productive Factor = 1 - Shrinkage %
Required HC

Estimated headcount needed to support the workload after shrinkage and operating assumptions.

Required HC = Workload Requirement ÷ Net Productive Capacity per HC
HC Gap

Difference between required HC and currently available productive HC.

HC Gap = Required HC - Available HC
Cost Impact

Estimated additional cost if extra HC is needed to close the staffing gap.

Cost Impact = HC Gap × Cost per HC
SLA Risk

An indicative output showing how far the scenario may move away from the target service level.

SLA Risk = function of demand pressure, HC gap, shrinkage, and AHT change

How to Use / SOP

User Guide
1
Set Baseline Assumptions

Enter your current volume, AHT, shrinkage, available HC, SLA target, occupancy, and cost per HC. This becomes the reference state.

2
Adjust Scenario Inputs

Update one or more drivers such as volume, AHT, shrinkage, available HC, or cost per HC to model the future state.

3
Run Simulation

Use the Run button to compare baseline and scenario and review changes in required HC, SLA risk, utilization, and cost impact.

4
Review Decision Outputs

Use the KPI cards, waterfall, HC comparison, and SLA risk meter to understand what is driving operational pressure.

5
Check Sensitivity Explorer

Review improvement levers such as lowering AHT, reducing shrinkage, or adding HC to identify the best recovery option.

6
Save or Export

Once the scenario is validated, save it for future comparison or export the view for leadership discussion and planning alignment.

Input Guidance

Use the same unit consistently across the dashboard. Volume should reflect the selected planning view, AHT should be entered in seconds, shrinkage should be entered as a percentage, and available HC should reflect productive headcount only.

This version includes premium horizontal scroll controls, drag-to-scroll, fixed-width KPI cards, fixed-width chart cards, and a formula glossary with SOP guidance so users can understand the dashboard and enter inputs correctly.

Decision commentary and action guidance

Scenario Summary

The current scenario increases workload demand while effective capacity stays constrained. Required HC rises from 152 to 168, pulling projected SLA down from 82.4% to 76.1%. This creates a high service risk and material monthly cost exposure if target performance must be protected.

Key Drivers

Volume increase is the strongest driver of staffing pressure.
Higher AHT compounds workload intensity and reduces throughput.
Shrinkage above 30% leaves limited recovery room for service stability.

Recommended Actions

Add 12 to 16 productive HC if target SLA must be maintained.
Reduce AHT by at least 15 seconds through coaching or process improvement.
Contain shrinkage below 29% to protect available capacity.
Use overtime as short-term containment only.
